Get enough sleep.

  • Create the right environment for better sleep.
  • On going lack of sleep or poor-quality sleep increases health problems like cardiovascular disease, high blood pressure, diabetes, depression and obesity.
  • Adults need between 7 and 9 hours of sleep per night. Babies, young children, and teens need even more sleep to enable their growth and development.

Benefits of drinking Water

  • Most of us dont drink enough water in our day life
  • But it’s best to start your day by re-hydrating with a full glass of water.
  • Drinking a water in the morning helps to aid digestion, enhance skin health and boost energy.
  • It helps fight against flu, Kidney stones, Loss weight and Heart attack

Reduce salt and sugar intake

  • A high salt intake can result in high blood pressure, and increase the risk of cardiovascular disease.

Some of Healthy fruits in our daily life

  • Apples : They are good source of vitamin C They’re rich in both soluble and insoluble fiber In fact, consuming apples regularly may lower your risk of heart disease, stroke, cancer, overweight, obesity.
  • Bananas : The benefits of bananas go beyond their potassium content. Along with providing 7% of the Daily Value(DV) for potassium, bananas contain (15Trusted Source):
    1. Vitamin B6 : 27% of the DV
    2. Vitamin C : 12% of the DV
    3. Magnesium : 8% of the DV
  • Pineapple : It is one of the most popular tropical fruits. One cup (165 grams) of pineapple provides 88% of the DV for vitamin C and 73% of the DV for manganese. Manganese supports metabolism and blood sugar regulation and acts as an antioxidant.
  • Mango : It is known as the “king of fruits,” mangoes are an excellent source of potassium, folate, fiber, and vitamins A, C, B6, E, and K.

Eat Fresh Vegetables

  • Vegetables are full of essential vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants that provide many important health benefits to your body.
    1. Improve blood pressure
    2. Help your eyes
    3. Improve your skin
    4. Reduce risk of heart disease
    5. Reduce risk of cancer
    6. Improve your immune health
  • Carrots : Carrots are known for being very high in vitamin A, which plays an important role in eye health, as you grow older.
  • Tomatoes : Each cup of chopped, raw tomatoes contains:
    1. 427 mg of potassium
    2. 24.7 mg of vitamin C
  • Onions : Each cup of chopped onions can provide vitamin C, vitamin B6, manganese
